EASTER

Easter is a Christian holiday commemorating the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, as described in the New Testament of the Bible. It is considered the most important event in Christian theology, symbolizing victory over sin and death.The date of Easter varies each year but typically falls on the first Sunday after the first full moon following the vernal equinox, marking the beginning of spring in the Northern Hemisphere.

History about Easter

Easter's origins are rooted in both Christian and pre-Christian traditions. The Christian celebration commemorates the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, a pivotal event in Christian theology. It is documented in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, and is considered the central event of Christianity.

More about Easter

Easter is associated with various symbols, such as the Easter egg, which represents new life and resurrection, and the Easter bunny, a symbol of fertility and rebirth.In addition to its religious significance, Easter is celebrated culturally with festivities like Easter egg hunts, egg decorating, and family gatherings. It is a time for reflection, renewal, and rejoicing, marking the culmination of the Lenten season and the beginning of a season of hope and new beginnings.
